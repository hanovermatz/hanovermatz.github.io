Evolutionary Immunology

I am an immunologist studying the fundamental principles of adaptive immunity. My work synthesizes molecular biology, immunology, and comparative evolutionary approaches to better understand the human immune system.

The adaptive immune system is the product of millions of years of evolution in jawed vertebrates. Investigating the dynamics of the immune system in different vertebrate species informs the fundamental principles governing immune protection, which can be optimized in the context of vaccine design, cancer, and autoimmunity.

Research Interests

  • B cells and antibodies
  • Evolution of adaptive immunity
  • Improving vaccine design

Research Highlights

Mechanisms of B cell biology

B cells produce antibodies that defend the host from infectious pathogens. Through the germinal center response, B cells undergo mutation, selection, and affinity maturation. Studying this process helps improve monoclonal antibody therapeutics and vaccines.

Evolution of B cell selection

Sharks are the oldest living organisms with an immune system like humans. My research demonstrated that fundamental principles of human germinal centers are present in the B cell selection sites of sharks, suggesting conserved evolutionary mechanisms.

Vaccine design and combating disease

What we learn from human and comparative studies informs how we modulate vaccines to induce durable immunity. As we improve vaccine design, we find new methods of combating infectious diseases that evolve in response to immune pressure.
